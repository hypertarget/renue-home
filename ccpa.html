California Privacy Notice (CCPA/CPRA)

Last updated: 2026 · Working template — please have qualified legal counsel review and finalize before relying on it.

This notice supplements our Privacy Policy and applies to California residents under the California Consumer Privacy Act, as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (collectively, “CCPA”). Terms used here have the meanings given in the CCPA.

Categories of personal information we collect

  • Identifiers — name, email, phone number, postal/project address, IP address, and online identifiers.
  • Customer records — contact details you submit with your request.
  • Commercial / project information — project type, ZIP code, timeframe, homeowner status, financing interest, and your answers.
  • Internet/network activity — interactions with the Site, pages viewed, and referring campaign data.
  • Geolocation — approximate location derived from ZIP/IP.
  • Inferences — drawn from the above to match you with relevant professionals.

We collect this information from you, automatically through the Site, and from marketing partners and data-verification providers. We do not knowingly collect sensitive personal information for the purpose of inferring characteristics, and we do not sell or share the personal information of consumers we know to be under 16.

Purposes

We use this information to provide the matching Service, communicate with you, operate and secure the Site, prevent fraud, perform marketing and analytics, and comply with law — as described in our Privacy Policy.

Disclosure, “sale,” and “sharing”

To provide the Service and operate our advertising, we disclose the categories above to: independent home-improvement professionals and companies; marketing and lead partners; advertising and analytics providers (via cookies/pixels); and our service providers. Under the CCPA, our disclosure of identifiers, commercial/project information, internet activity, geolocation, and inferences to marketing partners and home-improvement companies, and our use of cross-context advertising cookies, may constitute a “sale” or “sharing” of personal information.

Your California rights

  • Right to know/access the categories and specific pieces of personal information we collected.
  • Right to delete personal information we collected, subject to exceptions.
  • Right to correct inaccurate personal information.
  • Right to opt out of the sale or sharing of personal information.
  • Right to limit the use of sensitive personal information (to the extent we process any).
  • Right to non-discrimination for exercising your rights.

How to exercise your rights

To opt out of the sale/sharing of your personal information, use our Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information page, or enable the Global Privacy Control (GPC) in your browser — we honor GPC as a valid opt-out where required. For access, deletion, or correction requests, email privacy@renuehome.com. We will verify your identity as required by law. You may use an authorized agent, and you may appeal a decision by replying to our response. We aim to respond within the timeframes the CCPA requires.

“Shine the Light”

California Civil Code § 1798.83 permits California residents to request information about disclosures to third parties for their direct marketing purposes. To make such a request, contact privacy@renuehome.com.

Other states

Residents of other states with comprehensive privacy laws (such as Virginia, Colorado, Connecticut, Texas, Oregon, and others) may have similar rights. Use the same methods above to submit a request.
