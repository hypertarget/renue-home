Bathroom Remodel Cost in 2026

Updated June 2026

A bathroom remodel is one of the most common home projects, and it is also one of the hardest to price before you talk to anyone. The number swings a lot based on the size of the room, what you are changing, and the cost of labor in your area. Below are the current national ranges, pulled from published 2026 cost guides, so you can walk into a quote with a realistic budget instead of a guess.

Short version. The national average for a full bathroom remodel in 2026 is about $16,500, and most homeowners spend somewhere between $8,000 and $45,000 (Angi, This Old House). Smaller, focused jobs like a tub-to-shower swap cost far less.

Cost by the square foot

If you would rather think in price per square foot, budget remodels run about $80 to $120, mid-range projects land around $180 to $280, and luxury work can reach $500 per square foot (This Old House). A small hall bath at mid-range finishes therefore costs a lot less than a large primary suite, even when the work is similar.

Walk-in shower cost

A walk-in shower averages about $9,000, and most jobs come in between $6,000 and $12,000 (Angi). A simple prefab stall can run as little as $700 to $3,000, while a custom tiled shower with a glass enclosure usually runs $4,200 to $8,500. Remember to budget for permits, which often add $250 to $500, plus demolition and waterproofing.

Tub-to-shower conversion cost

Swapping a tub for a shower is one of the cheaper ways to refresh a bathroom. It averages around $3,000 and ranges from about $1,500 for a basic prefab unit up to $15,000 for natural stone and frameless glass (Angi, Home Depot). Labor alone is usually 40 to 60 percent of the total.

What drives the price

  • Scope. Moving plumbing or walls costs far more than a like-for-like swap. Keeping fixtures in place is the single biggest way to hold costs down.
  • Finishes. Stock vanities, fiberglass surrounds, and ceramic tile are budget friendly. Natural stone, custom cabinetry, and frameless glass move you toward the top of every range.
  • Labor in your area. Skilled trade rates vary by metro and they rose again in 2026. Industry guides put bathroom costs up roughly 4 to 6 percent over 2025, with plumbers and electricians leading the increase.
  • Size and access. A larger room means more material, and tight or upper-floor bathrooms add labor time.

Is it worth it

A mid-range bathroom remodel returns roughly 65 to 80 percent of its cost at resale, which makes it one of the stronger home-improvement investments (NerdWallet). Updates that improve safety and accessibility, like a curbless walk-in shower or grab bars, also help you stay in your home longer.

When you may not need a full remodel

If the layout works and the bones are sound, you can often get most of the visual win for a fraction of the cost. Refinishing a tub, replacing a vanity and faucet, new lighting, and fresh paint can modernize a room without touching the plumbing. Save the full remodel for when fixtures are failing, there is water damage, or the layout no longer fits how you live.
